Biscuits

Ingredients Measurements
Flour 2 cups all-purpose
Baking powder 1 tbs
Granulated sugar 1 tbs
Salt ½ tsp
Butter ⅓ cup (cold)
Milk 1 cup
Melted butter(optional) 2 tbs

Directions

  1. Yields 6-10 servings
  2. Preheat oven to 425 degrees F. Use an ungreased baking sheet or line it with parchment paper.
  3. In a large mixing bowl, stir together 2 cups flour, 1 tbs baking powder, 1 tbs granulated sugar, 1 tbs salt.
  4. Grate ⅓ cup butter (cold)and stir into the flour mixture.
  5. Gradually pour in 1 cup milk or buttermilk while stirring until dough just comes together.Avoid overmixing to keep the biscuits tender.
  6. Turn the dough onto a clean, lightly floured countertop. Gather it into a ball and press down until it is about 1 inch thick.
  7. Use a circle biscuit cutter or the rim of a drinking glass to cut out your biscuits. Collect any dough scraps, put them back together and continue cutting until all the dough is used.
  8. Place the cut biscuits onto the baking sheet. Bake in the preheated oven for 12-15 minutes or until the tops are golden brown. The size of your biscuits might impact the cooking time slightly so watch closely.
  9. Remove from the oven and brush the tops with melted butter for added flavor and a glossy finish.
  10. Serve and Enjoy!!!
